# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist for College Students

## General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account** — Combine uppercase letters,
   numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager (like Bitwarden, which is free) rather
   than reusing the same password across sites.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts** — Gmail, GitHub,
   LinkedIn, and banking apps all support 2FA. This stops attackers even if they know
   your password.

3. **Never click on unknown links in SMS, WhatsApp, or email** — Fraudulent links often
   look almost identical to real sites (e.g., "sbi-netbanking-verify.xyz"). Always type
   the official URL yourself or use bookmarks.

4. **Keep your phone and apps updated** — Security patches in updates fix known
   vulnerabilities that attackers exploit. Enable automatic updates on your device.

5. **Do not share OTPs with anyone — ever** — No bank, government body, or company
   will ever ask for your OTP over a call. If someone asks for it, it is a scam.

6. **Log out of accounts on shared/public computers** — College labs, cyber cafes, and
   library computers are shared. Always log out and never save passwords in the browser.

---

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety (CRITICAL for Indian Students)

7. **Understand the difference between "Send" and "Collect" on UPI** — Entering your
   UPI PIN always means MONEY LEAVES your account. There is no scenario where you
   need to enter your PIN to RECEIVE money. If someone claims otherwise, it is fraud.

8. **Never scan a QR code sent by a buyer/stranger to "receive" payment** — Scanning
   a QR code can initiate a payment FROM you. Sellers never need to scan anything to
   receive money.

---

## Social & Identity Safety

9. **Do not share personal documents (Aadhaar, PAN, student ID) on unverified
   platforms** — Once shared, they can be misused for identity theft, fake SIM cards,
   or loan fraud.

10. **Verify job and scholarship offers independently** — Search the company name
    separately. Legitimate recruiters never ask for a "registration fee" or "security deposit."

---

## Reporting Cybercrime

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 (24x7, toll-free, available across India)
- **For financial fraud:** Call your bank's helpline immediately to freeze the transaction.
  Time is critical — report within minutes for the best chance of recovery.
