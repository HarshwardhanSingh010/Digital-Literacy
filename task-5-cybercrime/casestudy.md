# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
## Prepared by: Harshwardhan Singh Panwar | Reg. No.: 25MIM10042

---

## What is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of financial cybercrime where attackers
deceive victims into authorising money transfers or revealing sensitive credentials through
psychological manipulation, fake links, or impersonation.

---

## Realistic Case Study: The "Collect Request" Trap

**Scenario:**

Rohan, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, had listed his old laptop
for sale on an online classifieds platform. He received a message from someone claiming
to be an Army officer posted in Pune who was "urgently" interested in buying the laptop.

The buyer said he would pay via PhonePe. A few minutes later, Rohan received a UPI
notification — but instead of a "Pay" request, it was a **Collect request** (a request
asking Rohan to authorise a payment TO the buyer). The buyer, meanwhile, was on call
with Rohan and said, "I've sent the money — just approve it on your app."

Confused and trusting, Rohan entered his UPI PIN. Instead of receiving money, ₹18,500
was debited from his account within seconds.

---

## How It Happens — Step by Step

1. Attacker finds a target (seller on OLX/Quikr, someone in a help group, etc.)
2. Attacker builds false trust (claims a respected identity — army, doctor, officer)
3. Attacker sends a **UPI Collect Request** disguised verbally as a "payment"
4. Victim is kept on call to feel urgency and reduce thinking time
5. Victim approves the request, entering their UPI PIN
6. Money is instantly transferred to the attacker's account
7. Attacker disconnects and blocks the victim

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- Students and young adults selling items on classified platforms
- People receiving job offers or scholarship announcements
- Small business owners and freelancers
- Anyone unfamiliar with how UPI Collect Requests work

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (often ₹5,000 – ₹2,00,000)
- Emotional distress, shame, and loss of trust in digital payments
- Difficulty recovering funds (UPI transactions are near-instant and hard to reverse)
- Possible identity theft if personal documents were shared
- Legal complications if the victim unknowingly forwarded money (mule account scenario)
