# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
## Prepared by: Harshwardhan Singh Panwar | Reg. No.: 25MIM10042

---

## DO's ✅

1. **Do keep your profile professional** — Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a
   bio that reflects your academic interests and goals.

2. **Do share your learning and achievements** — Post about certifications, projects,
   coding challenges completed, or workshops attended. This builds your digital portfolio.

3. **Do think before you post** — Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor
   or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.

4. **Do use strong, unique passwords** — Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all
   social media accounts to prevent unauthorised access.

5. **Do engage respectfully in discussions** — Comment thoughtfully. Disagreeing is fine;
   being disrespectful is not. Online conversations are permanent.

6. **Do review your privacy settings regularly** — Limit who can see your personal
   information, location, and contact details.

7. **Do cite sources when sharing information** — Always credit the original creator when
   reposting articles, images, or ideas.

---

## DON'Ts ❌

1. **Don't share personal or sensitive information publicly** — Avoid posting your phone
   number, home address, Aadhaar number, UPI ID, or college ID on any social platform.

2. **Don't post or share unverified information** — Spreading misinformation or fake news
   can have serious legal and social consequences in India (IT Act, 2000).

3. **Don't engage with or spread cyberbullying** — Do not share, like, or comment on
   content that mocks, harasses, or demeans any individual or group.

4. **Don't use college or office Wi-Fi for personal social media if unsure of its
   security** — Public and shared networks can expose your login credentials.

5. **Don't post in anger or frustration** — Emotional posts made in haste are often
   regretted later and can damage your personal and professional reputation permanently.

6. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from unknown people** — Fake profiles are
   commonly used for phishing, scams, and social engineering attacks.

7. **Don't plagiarise content** — Reposting someone else's work as your own is both
   unethical and potentially illegal under copyright law.
